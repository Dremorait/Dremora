Internship Program

Course Guidelines

Everything you need to know about Dremora's internship program — structure, expectations, conduct, and how we help you succeed.

Last Updated: May 2026

1. Program Overview

The Dremora IT Internship Program is a real-world, hands-on learning experience designed for aspiring software developers, designers, and tech enthusiasts. Unlike typical internship programs, at Dremora you work on live production projects — building actual systems that impact real users.

Duration: 1 to 3 months (flexible, based on project)
Mode: Remote / Hybrid (Pune-based candidates may opt for on-site)
Stipend: Performance-based (details shared during onboarding)
Certification: Completion certificate issued upon successful program completion

2. Eligibility & Selection

To be eligible for Dremora's internship program:

  • You must be currently enrolled in or have completed a degree in Computer Science, IT, Engineering, Design, or a related field.
  • You must have at least foundational knowledge of programming (any language), web development, or UI/UX design.
  • You must be able to commit to the agreed number of hours per week (minimum 20 hrs/week).
  • You must complete a brief technical assessment or portfolio review as part of the selection process.

Selection is merit-based. We evaluate applicants on problem-solving ability, learning attitude, and communication skills — not just technical scores.

3. Program Structure

The internship is structured into three phases:

  • Phase 1 — Onboarding (Week 1): Introduction to Dremora's tech stack, tools, workflow, and project codebase. Setup development environment and complete orientation tasks.
  • Phase 2 — Core Development (Weeks 2–8): Work on assigned features, modules, or projects under mentor guidance. Weekly reviews and code walkthroughs.
  • Phase 3 — Capstone & Review (Final 2 weeks): Complete a capstone module, conduct peer reviews, and present your work to the team.

4. Technical Standards

All interns are expected to maintain the following technical standards:

  • Write clean, commented, and readable code following Dremora's internal coding conventions.
  • Use version control (Git) for all work — commit messages must be clear and descriptive.
  • Conduct self-review before submitting any pull request or deliverable.
  • Attend all scheduled technical reviews and stand-up meetings.
  • Test your code before submission — untested code will be sent back for revision.
  • Never push directly to the main branch without mentor approval.

5. Code of Conduct

Dremora fosters a culture of respect, growth, and professionalism. All interns must:

  • Treat all team members, mentors, and clients with respect and professionalism at all times.
  • Maintain confidentiality of all internal projects, client data, and proprietary business information.
  • Be honest about progress — if you're stuck, raise it early rather than staying silent.
  • Avoid plagiarism — all code and design must be original or properly attributed.
  • Not share access credentials, internal documents, or project assets with external parties.
  • Report any issues, conflicts, or concerns to the mentor or program lead promptly.

6. Attendance & Communication

  • Interns are expected to maintain at least 80% attendance in scheduled meetings and check-ins.
  • Inform your mentor at least 24 hours in advance if you cannot attend a scheduled session.
  • Respond to messages on official communication channels (WhatsApp/Slack/Email) within 4 working hours during business days.
  • Daily stand-up updates are required in the intern group — brief summary of what you did, what you plan to do, and any blockers.

7. Evaluation & Certification

Interns are evaluated on the following criteria at the end of the program:

  • Technical Quality (40%): Code quality, problem-solving approach, and technical understanding.
  • Project Contribution (30%): Actual features built, bugs fixed, and impact delivered.
  • Professionalism (20%): Punctuality, communication, teamwork, and conduct.
  • Learning & Growth (10%): Demonstrable improvement from start to end of the program.

Interns scoring 70% or above receive a completion certificate and letter of recommendation. Outstanding performers may be offered a full-time or extended role at Dremora.

8. Termination from Program

Dremora reserves the right to terminate an intern's participation in the program due to:

  • Repeated violation of the Code of Conduct.
  • Persistent non-attendance or non-responsiveness without valid reason.
  • Breach of confidentiality or sharing of internal information.
  • Plagiarism, dishonesty, or submission of copied work.

In such cases, no certificate will be issued, and the intern will be removed from all internal systems immediately.
